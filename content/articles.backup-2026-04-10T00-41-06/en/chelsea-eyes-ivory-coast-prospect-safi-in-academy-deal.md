Chelsea have opened talks with the prestigious Right to Dream Academy in the Ivory Coast over the acquisition of promising forward N'Sira Safi, as the Blues intensify their scouting efforts across West Africa. The London club's interest in the young striker reflects their strategic focus on identifying elite prospects early in their development, with Safi identified as a player with significant potential to develop at their world-class facilities.

The Right to Dream Academy, renowned for producing polished young talents who transition smoothly to European football, has become an increasingly important hunting ground for top clubs. If the deal materializes, Safi would join Chelsea's burgeoning academy system, where he would compete for development alongside other promising young players. The move would represent another notable addition to Chelsea's ambitious long-term recruitment strategy, which has seen them prioritize young, high-potential signings over the past seasons.
