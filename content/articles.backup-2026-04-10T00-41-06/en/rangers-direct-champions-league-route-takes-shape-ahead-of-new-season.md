Rangers fans have a tantalizing prospect to consider as the new campaign approaches: a potential bypass of the grueling Champions League qualifiers. The Scottish champions could skip the preliminary rounds entirely and parachute directly into the group stage, but success hinges on two critical conditions. Steven Gerrard's side must firstly reclaim the Premiership crown from Celtic, then benefit from an Olympiakos stumble in Greece. Currently, Greece's coefficient ranking sits just below Scotland's, meaning that if the Greek champions fail to deliver domestically, UEFA's regulations could elevate Scotland's representative straight into the lucrative group stage.

This scenario offers Rangers a significant competitive and financial advantage. The qualifier gauntlet typically demands three rounds of matches—thousands of kilometers traveled and squad rotation risks—before reaching the group stage. For a club with European ambitions, avoiding this minefield would preserve energy and resources during a congested fixture list. However, Rangers cannot rely on hoping Olympiakos slip up; the focus must remain on dethroning Celtic domestically, a task that will demand unwavering consistency and quality throughout the campaign.
