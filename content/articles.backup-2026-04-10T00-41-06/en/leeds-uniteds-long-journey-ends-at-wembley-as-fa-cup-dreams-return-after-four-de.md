The Yorkshire giants have finally broken through their longest FA Cup drought, dispatching West Ham in a thrilling encounter to book their place in the semi-finals. It marks a remarkable turnaround for a club that has endured 39 years without reaching this stage of English football's oldest competition. Leeds supporters, many of whom have never witnessed their side contest a final, now dare to dream of ending the club's 20-year trophy drought. The victory over West Ham was no mere formality—it required drama, determination, and the kind of character that defines a genuine cup run.

Standing between Leeds and a Wembley final is one more hurdle, but the momentum is undeniably on their side. After years of rebuilding and false dawns, the club's hierarchy and fanbase sense something special brewing. With Wembley's iconic arch in sight, Leeds have the chance to prove they belong among the elite once more. For a club whose last major trophy came in the Championship in 2004, this opportunity represents far more than just another cup run—it's validation that Leeds' ambitions to reclaim their place among England's biggest sides are within reach.
