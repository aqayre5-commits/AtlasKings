The potential windfall hinges on UEFA's coefficient rankings, which determine how many teams from each league qualify directly for the group stage without playing qualifying rounds. Scotland's coefficient could rise significantly if Rangers win the domestic title while Olympiakos—historically one of Europe's stronger performers—finish outside the top spot in Greece. This shift would elevate Scottish football's standing in European calculations, allowing Rangers to bypass the grueling qualifier gauntlet that typically awaits British teams.

For Rangers fans, the implications are substantial. Winning the Premiership remains the priority, but this scenario offers additional motivation: a direct route to Europe's elite competition would provide financial benefits, fixture congestion relief, and a stronger platform for challenging in the group stage itself. However, Rangers cannot control Olympiakos's domestic performance, meaning they must focus entirely on delivering the Scottish title while hoping European football's bureaucratic machinery works in their favor.
