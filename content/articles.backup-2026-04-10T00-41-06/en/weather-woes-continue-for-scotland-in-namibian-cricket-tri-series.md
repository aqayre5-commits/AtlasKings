Scotland's campaign in Namibia has been hampered by persistent rain for the second time in as many outings, leaving the Scottish side frustrated in their ICC Men's Cricket World Cup League 2 Tri-Series fixtures. The unpredictable weather conditions have disrupted their rhythm and prevented them from building momentum in what is a crucial league phase ahead of the 2025 World Cup. For a team looking to stake their claim in ODI cricket, weather interruptions feel particularly costly when matches are being decided by shortened formats or washouts.

The rain delays have become a recurring headache for Scotland's management and playing XI, who came to Namibia with clear objectives in the tri-series competition. With World Cup qualification on the line, every match carries significant weight, making weather-enforced stoppages even more frustrating. Scotland will hope for clearer skies in their remaining fixtures and a chance to showcase their cricket on a proper playing surface.
