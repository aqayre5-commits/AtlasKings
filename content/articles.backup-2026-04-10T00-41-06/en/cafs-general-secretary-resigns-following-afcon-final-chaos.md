Veron Mosengo-Omba has announced his resignation as CAF general secretary, marking another turbulent chapter for the Confederation of African Football. His departure comes in the wake of the chaotic Afcon final, which saw organizational failures that left fans, players, and nations questioning the competence of African football's leadership. The controversial administrator, whose tenure has been marked by repeated crises and poor decision-making, exits at a critical moment when the continent's football needs stability and credibility.

The Afcon final debacle—marked by security breaches, logistical nightmares, and safety concerns—became the final straw for many stakeholders. Mosengo-Omba's resignation, while potentially overdue in the eyes of critics, raises urgent questions about how CAF will rebuild confidence ahead of future tournaments. African football fans and clubs will be hoping his successor brings the professionalism and vision needed to restore the tournament's reputation and ensure such embarrassing scenes don't become the norm.
