After a grueling quarter-final battle that went to extra time, Leeds United finally broke through their decades-long FA Cup curse when Pascal Struijk stepped up to deliver the decisive spot-kick in the penalty shootout against West Ham. The moment represented far more than a simple cup progression—it signaled the end of a 39-year drought for the Yorkshire club, which hasn't reached an FA Cup semi-final since the Thatcher era. For a fanbase that has endured years of Championship football and near-misses in recent seasons, Struijk's composed finish sent waves of euphoria through Elland Road.

The defender's cool demeanor under pressure epitomized Leeds' determination throughout the tie. While both sides had chances in normal time, the match's intensity only grew as it stretched into extra time, making the shootout a tense affair where one mistake could prove fatal. When Struijk struck his penalty past the West Ham goalkeeper, he didn't just score a goal—he wrote himself into Leeds folklore and reignited hopes of silverware for a club hungry to return to their former glories.
