Arsenal's unexpected FA Cup defeat has sparked serious questions about the Gunners' ability to compete on multiple fronts this season. The Monday Night Club panel examined the implications of the loss, with opinions split on whether this represents a minor stumble or a more troubling pattern. For a club with Premier League title aspirations, dropping out of a competition they could realistically win adds pressure on their domestic campaign—they cannot afford to lose focus on their main objective while juggling European commitments.

The consensus among analysts suggests the real damage lies not in the exit itself, but in how it happened. Arsenal's performance raised concerns about consistency and mental resilience when facing adversity. With rivals Manchester City and Liverpool circling, the Gunners need to prove they can bounce back quickly and maintain their intensity across all competitions. The coming weeks will be crucial in determining whether this setback galvanizes Mikel Arteta's side or becomes the first crack in an otherwise promising season.
