The prospect of all four British home nations competing in the same World Cup remains one of football's rarest achievements. With England and Scotland already booked their tickets to North America, attention now turns to Wales and Northern Ireland, who face do-or-die European qualifying playoffs next March. Should both nations prevail, it would mark only the fourth time in World Cup history that England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland have all qualified for the tournament—a feat last accomplished in 1958.

Wales enters their playoff as heavy favorites against a lower-ranked opponent, but Northern Ireland face a significantly tougher challenge. The Irish side's route to qualification is far from guaranteed, adding genuine suspense to their March showdown. For Moroccan fans and neutrals alike, the narrative of the home nations' qualification battles adds another compelling subplot to the 2026 tournament's build-up, showcasing how even established football nations must fight for their place on the world stage.
