Bernardo Silva's tenure at Manchester City appears to be reaching a critical juncture, with Pep Lijnders dropping hints that the Portuguese playmaker may seek a new challenge this summer. The 29-year-old midfielder has been a cornerstone of City's success under Guardiola, but his playing time has become increasingly limited in recent seasons. Lijnders' cryptic comments suggest the club's hierarchy may be open to offers for Silva, marking a potential end to a six-year relationship that has yielded multiple Premier League titles and domestic cups.

The timing of these remarks comes as City prepare for a potential squad overhaul, with several aging stars facing uncertain futures. Silva's departure would represent a significant shift in City's midfield dynamics, potentially opening doors for younger talents to step up.
