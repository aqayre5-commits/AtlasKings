The CAF chief's dual diplomatic tour underscores the deepening crisis surrounding the AFCON final arrangements. In Senegal, Motsepe addressed serious corruption allegations affecting the continental body, signaling the need for accountability. However, his visit to Morocco took on a notably different tone, with the focus shifting toward CAF's institutional relationship with the kingdom and the urgent need to overhaul competition regulations. The contrasting approaches suggest Motsepe is working to manage fallout on multiple fronts as the dispute threatens to damage African football's credibility.

The political and legal implications of the AFCON final controversy continue to reverberate across the continent. Morocco's football community will be watching closely to see how CAF addresses governance reforms and whether new competition rules can prevent similar disputes in future tournaments. With both nations at the center of the storm, the resolution of this crisis will test CAF's ability to operate fairly and transparently—something Moroccan fans and administrators are clearly demanding from the continental federation.
