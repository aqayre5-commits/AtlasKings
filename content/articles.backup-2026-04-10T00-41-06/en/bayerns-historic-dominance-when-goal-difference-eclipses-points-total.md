Bayern Munich are putting on a masterclass in dominance this season, and the numbers tell a stunning story. With 70 points accumulated from 22 wins and just one loss, the Bavarian giants have posted an extraordinary +72 goal difference—meaning they're scoring goals at a rate that nearly matches their points total. That single defeat to Augsburg aside, Bayern's average victory margin sits at a staggering 3.32 goals, creating a rare statistical anomaly that begs the question: has any champion ever finished with a goal difference actually exceeding their points haul?

The historical record suggests this would be unprecedented. While Rangers (1898-99) came closest with a ratio of 0.388—meaning their goal difference was nearly 39% of their points total—no champion has ever achieved the inverse. Bayern would need a goal difference surpassing 70 to make history, and while their current trajectory is breathtaking, it underscores just how exceptional such dominance would be. For context, even Ajax's dominant 1966-67 Eredivisie title-winning side only managed a 0.176 ratio. Bayern's current campaign isn't just about winning; it's about redefining what competitive superiority looks like in modern football.
