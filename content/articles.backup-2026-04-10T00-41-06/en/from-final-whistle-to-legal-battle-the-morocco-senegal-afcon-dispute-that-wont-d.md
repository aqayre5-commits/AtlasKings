The 2021 Africa Cup of Nations final between Morocco and Senegal was supposed to be a celebration of African football at its finest. Instead, the match in Casablanca became the starting point for months of controversy. Senegal secured a 4-2 victory on penalties after a 1-1 draw, but what followed was far from a gracious acceptance of defeat. Morocco's federation immediately lodged formal complaints with CAF, citing alleged irregularities during the match. Fan incidents, including arrests following the final, further inflamed tensions between the two nations' supporters and officials.

The dispute has since exploded beyond the pitch into the corridors of power. CAF initially dismissed Morocco's appeals, but the Atlas Lions refused to accept the outcome, escalating their case to the Court of Arbitration for Sport in Switzerland. Meanwhile, calls for boycotts have emerged on social media, and diplomatic tensions have simmered beneath the surface. The case has become less about football and more about national pride, with both federations digging in their heels. For supporters on both sides, what began as a thrilling continental competition has transformed into an ongoing nightmare that refuses to reach closure.
