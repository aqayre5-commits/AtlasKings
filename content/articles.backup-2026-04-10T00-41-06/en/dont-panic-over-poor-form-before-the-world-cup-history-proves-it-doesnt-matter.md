When the United States hosted the World Cup in 1994, their preparations looked anything but impressive. Playing 12 matches between January and April while in a full-time training camp, the Americans managed just two victories — a record so poor it even included a shock defeat to Iceland, a nation barely on the global football map at the time. By all measures, the run-in was a disaster that would have sent modern fans into a panic on social media.

Yet that same "disappointing" team went on to exceed expectations in the tournament itself. They navigated the group stage and came agonizingly close to a semifinal berth, falling only to eventual champions Brazil in the Round of 16 by a single goal. Their success proved what many in football often forget: the weeks leading up to a World Cup tell you remarkably little about what happens when the tournament actually begins. Tournament football is a different beast entirely — one that rewards freshness, focus, and peaking at exactly the right moment rather than being in form months beforehand.

For any national team currently struggling in their World Cup preparations, the message is clear: there's still everything to play for.
