The Pittodrie faithful are holding their breath as Aberdeen battles to avoid an unthinkable drop down the Scottish Premiership ladder. Once considered one of Scotland's "Big Two" alongside Rangers and Celtic, the Dons now find themselves in a precarious position where every point feels like a lifeline. Manager Barry Robson's side knows that slipping into the Championship would represent a seismic shift in the club's recent history, making their remaining fixtures do-or-die encounters that will define the entire season.

The desperation isn't confined to the top flight either. Across Scotland's lower leagues, promotion and relegation battles are reaching fever pitch, with teams fighting tooth and nail for survival or the chance to climb back to respectability. For the relegated sides looking to bounce back and those chasing promotion dreams, these final weeks represent everything they've worked for all season. It's squeaky bum time indeed—where margins are razor-thin and one bad result can derail entire campaigns.
