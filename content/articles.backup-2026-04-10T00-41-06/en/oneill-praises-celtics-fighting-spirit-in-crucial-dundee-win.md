Celtic maintained their title challenge with a hard-fought victory at Dundee, with interim manager Martin O'Neill highlighting his squad's character and determination in what proved a testing encounter. The Glasgow giants showed the grit required to grind out results in a competitive league, refusing to wilt despite facing a stubborn opposition side. For O'Neill, the performance demonstrated exactly the mentality needed if Celtic are to sustain their challenge at the business end of the season.

"This is what it takes to win titles," O'Neill reflected after the match, emphasizing that championship-winning teams must deliver on difficult days when football isn't flowing freely. The interim manager's comments suggest Celtic are building the psychological strength necessary to compete for honors, a crucial ingredient alongside tactical prowess and individual quality. With every point precious in the title race, victories forged through sheer determination rather than pure dominance often prove the most valuable come May.
