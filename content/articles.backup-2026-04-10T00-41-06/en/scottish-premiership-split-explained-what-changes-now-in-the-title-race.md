The Scottish Premiership operates uniquely compared to other European leagues, dividing its 38-game season into two distinct phases. After each team plays 33 matches, the league splits into two groups of six clubs based on their final standings. The top six teams continue competing for the title in the Championship Group, while the bottom six battle to avoid relegation in the Relegation Play-off Group. This format adds drama and unpredictability, as teams can still mathematically alter their fate during the second half of the season.

For title contenders like Celtic and Rangers, the split represents a critical juncture. The top six teams carry their accumulated points forward into the Championship Group, where they play an additional five matches against each other. This means every point earned before the split becomes vital—dropped points early cannot be recovered once the league divides. The intensity amplifies as rivals face each other multiple times in quick succession, making the run-in a pressure cooker for Scotland's biggest clubs and ensuring the title race remains compelling until the final whistle.
