AS FAR Rabat has seized the upper hand in an all-Moroccan CAF Champions League semi-final, defeating RS Berkane 2-0 at Prince Moulay Abdellah Stadium in Rabat on Saturday. The first-leg victory gives the capital side a substantial advantage heading into the return fixture, positioning them one step closer to continental silverware. For a club steeped in Morocco's football history, this is the kind of result that matters—not flashy, but decisive, built on composure and clinical finishing when it counted.

The match unfolded in two distinct halves. For much of the opening 45 minutes, both sides remained cautious, each clearly aware of the magnitude of the occasion. Neither team pressed aggressively in the early going, content to feel out the opposition and avoid gifting possession in dangerous areas. This measured approach is typical of knockout football at this level, where a single mistake can determine a tie. AS FAR controlled territory without creating the clear-cut chances that might have settled the contest early, while Berkane defended compactly and looked to exploit space on the counter when opportunities arose.

The breakthrough came in the 58th minute, when AS FAR finally converted their pressure into a goal. That opening changed the complexion entirely. Once ahead, AS FAR's confidence visibly grew, and the team's movement became sharper and more purposeful. A second goal followed, sealing what amounts to a near-decisive advantage. In the context of a two-legged tie, a 2-0 home victory is substantial. Berkane will need to produce something extraordinary in the return leg—they must not only win but win by a margin that carries them through on away goals or aggregate, a mountain to climb against a side that has already proven its mettle at this stage of the competition.

The semi-final pits two of Morocco's most successful clubs against each other, and that rivalry adds another layer to proceedings. AS FAR, based in Rabat, carries the prestige of multiple domestic titles and continental experience. Berkane, from the northern city of Nador, has emerged in recent seasons as a genuine force in Moroccan football, but reaching the CAF Champions League semi-final represents perhaps the pinnacle of their ambitions to date. For them, the path back into this tie now requires not just a win but something close to a reversal of Saturday's narrative.

The winner of this semi-final will face either Al Ahly or another opponent in the final, depending on the outcome of the other semi. For Moroccan football, there is prestige in having a representative in African club football's showpiece event, and both AS FAR and Berkane would relish the opportunity. For now, AS FAR holds the initiative, and when you control a semi-final tie at home with two away goals against you, you are very much the favorites. Berkane's second leg will tell us whether they have the resilience to force a different outcome.
