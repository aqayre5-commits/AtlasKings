Achraf Hakimi is playing with the attacking verve that has defined his best moments at Paris Saint-Germain, according to L'Équipe's assessment this week. The Moroccan international has been instrumental in PSG's creative output as the club navigates the business end of the UEFA Champions League, with his performance against Liverpool on Wednesday exemplifying the form that makes him essential to the club's European ambitions. Alongside left-back Nuno Mendes, Hakimi has become a primary engine of PSG's attacking play, operating from the right flank with the kind of pace and incision that unsettles defences in continental competition.

For Hakimi, this resurgence arrives at a moment when consistency has sometimes eluded him since his arrival in Paris. The 26-year-old brought a proven pedigree from Inter Milan, where he had been a cornerstone of Antonio Conte's Serie A title-winning side, combining defensive solidity with explosive attacking instinct. At PSG, however, his first seasons saw him rotate between brilliant cameos and stretches where his impact seemed muted by the club's sometimes disjointed tactical framework. The weight of expectation at a club of PSG's financial power and continental ambition can obscure steady performers; what L'Équipe's observation reflects is not a dramatic transformation, but rather a player settling into the rhythm required to deliver his best work when it matters most.

The Champions League's knockout phases demand something different from full-backs than domestic league football does. Hakimi's ability to operate both as a traditional attacking outlet and as a more cautious defensive presence—depending on opposition and moment—becomes more valuable when margins tighten and European sides expose tactical weaknesses with clinical efficiency. Against Liverpool, a side that generates significant attacking play from their own full-back positions, Hakimi's contribution was not merely about attacking returns but about controlling space and transitions. His reinvigorated form suggests PSG's coaching staff has found the right balance in deploying him, allowing his natural running style and crossing ability to flourish while maintaining the defensive discipline that European nights demand.

For Morocco, Hakimi remains a figure of considerable national importance. The defender has been a cornerstone of the Atlas Lions' recent ascendancy, particularly during their remarkable 2022 World Cup run and their steady progression in Africa Cup of Nations and World Cup qualifiers. His form at club level directly impacts his availability and sharpness for the national team. PSG's progression deeper into the Champions League also provides Morocco with an additional lens through which to monitor one of its most important players on the international stage, especially as the next African Cup of Nations approaches.

What happens next will depend on PSG's draw and fixture schedule, but Hakimi's rediscovered confidence arrives at precisely the moment when the club will need its best players performing at their ceiling. The Champions League's knockout rounds tolerate no room for passengers; for a side with PSG's resources and expectations, Hakimi playing with his full range of abilities is not a luxury but a necessity.
